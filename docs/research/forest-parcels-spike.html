Capabilities are land, in three voices

Each capability is a parcel of ground — tint = its status (green / dry pale-grass / withered brown), and its vegetation density IS its test count: a well-proven plot grows thick garden-forest, a barely-tested one sits sparse. Parcels wear one of three surface themes — meadow, woodland, heath — each designed by its own subagent, so the island reads as varied country, not one repeated texture. Nothing is decoration.

UAT criteria = the lantern walk (kept)

The story's UAT test criteria line the trail to the tree: gold glow = proven, unlit = pending, red = failing. The lanterns ARE the count — no extra badge (the nameplate seal was cut; the walk carries the signal alone). The human-witness signpost keeps its seal at the trailhead, and the proof walk stays the most prominent thing on the island.

Buildings: one subagent each

The cottage ("U") and tower ("NE") identity stamps were each designed by a dedicated subagent under a shared style bible — flat cel-shaded 2.5D (a second face in a darker tone, no gradients), with expression: chimney smoke, lit windows, banners. This is the swarm pattern scaled down: at full scale, every building bucket gets its own designer.

Lighting: parked, deliberately

No open-source lighting engine exists for SVG scenes worth standing on — real light means a renderer shift: three.js/R3F (a spike already lives in this repo for the website) or PixiJS + @pixi/lights. That's its own future arc + ADR; this arc stays flat, and the cel-shaded 2.5D above needs none of it.
